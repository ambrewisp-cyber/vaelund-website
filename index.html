Lore
The Hollow was sealed two hundred years ago. The Six Houses agreed on nothing else, but they agreed on that. Something had to be contained — something that ate the world from the inside out, island by island, until the sky turned silver and the grass started growing wrong.
The seals are weakening. No one is sure why. The Archons are dead or missing. The old texts are incomplete or encrypted or written in languages that no one teaching anymore. You arrived on the first boat in forty years.
What you're walking into

One world, no loading screens

Walk from Millhaven to the Hollow's edge without a transition. 150x150 world tiles. Dungeons that aren't instanced. The danger is real because the world is shared.

Combat that has weight

Every swing means something. Mobs that react, stagger, flank, and flee. Damage that accumulates. Death that costs time. Builds that define your playstyle, not just your numbers.

Quests that connect

A main arc with real stakes. Side stories that tie back to it. Factions with conflicting agendas. Finish one thing and another thing changes. The world is not a checklist.

No hand-holding

No quest markers. No guided path. Dungeons you navigate by looking. Secrets for people who actually look. A world that does not explain itself to you.

Abilities, not stats

Spell combos. Positional effects. Cooldowns you manage. Stamina you ration. Your build defines how you fight — not just how hard you hit.

The Hollow grows

Not a raid event. A slow apocalypse. The seals are weakening. This has been happening for thirty years. No one has fixed it yet.
